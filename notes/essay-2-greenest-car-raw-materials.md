# Essay 2 Raw Materials

## Title

- `The Greenest Car in India Is the One Already Built`

## Core thesis

The biggest near-term climate lever in Indian mobility is extending the useful life of vehicles whose embodied carbon has already been paid, while building the trust systems that make reuse credible.

## Strongest arguments

- reuse is not a fallback climate strategy; it is the fastest realistic lever in the category
- the used-EV problem is a confidence and information problem before it is a marketing problem
- battery transparency, transfer ease, warranty logic, and tax structure shape EV resale more than generic pro-EV rhetoric
- climate and trust are linked: a reused vehicle only helps if the buyer can trust the underlying asset

## Best attachment-backed additions

- the strongest essay-2 line is now clearer:
  - India's used-EV market is forming at structurally wrong prices because every participant is pricing the battery on different assumptions
- best value-destruction numbers:
  - 3-5-year-old Nexon EVs trading at `45-50%` of original value against an OEM-implied `55-60%` floor
  - `Rs 60,000-Rs 90,000` gap per vehicle
  - `Rs 300-450 crore` of value destruction at `50,000` annual used-EV transactions by FY28
- best battery / standards points:
  - battery is roughly `40%` of EV value
  - Geotab longitudinal work across `22,700` EVs and `21` models found average annual capacity loss of `1.8-2.3%`
  - SoH measurement methods can vary by up to `+/-7` percentage points on the same battery
  - the missing layer is a chemistry-specific SoH standard plus BPAN and VAHAN integration
- best financing / insurance points:
  - EV loans carry LTV ratios `10-30%` lower than ICE equivalents
  - tenures are `6-18` months shorter
  - EMI burden is `1.5-1.8x`
  - used EV dealer margins are taxed at `18%` while new EVs sit at `5%`
  - an SoH-banded IDV framework and battery-rider products are the right insurance logic
- best warranty / experience points:
  - warranty transfer is usually allowed but still manual, OEM-specific, and poorly surfaced
  - warranty status should become a portable, verified data field
  - apartment charging NOC standardised to `7 days` and CCS-2 mandate from FY27 make the category more usable

## Best channel-derived lines of thought

- battery health needs to become a standard, portable fact
- EV resale price discovery improves when transfer friction drops
- the category needs recertification logic, not just charging-point optimism
- policy that reduces transfer and tax friction can unlock climate outcomes indirectly
