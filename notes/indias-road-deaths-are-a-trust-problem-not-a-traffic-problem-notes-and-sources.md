# Notes and Sources: India's Road Deaths Are a Trust Problem, Not a Traffic Problem

These notes support the essay [`India's Road Deaths Are a Trust Problem, Not a Traffic Problem`](/Users/vikram/Documents/New%20project%204/notes/indias-road-deaths-are-a-trust-problem-not-a-traffic-problem.md). They are organized around the main factual claims in the piece.

The essay cites the official figure of `172,890` road deaths in India in `2023`.

Source:

## 8. Additional compensation metrics

The essay includes several additional compensation-system metrics:
- `70%` of low-income families unaware compensation exists
- `<5%` hit-and-run compensation access
- roughly `11 lakh` MACT cases pending
- roughly `986 crore` in MACT-linked bank accounts across five states

Source:
- Crashfree India compensation planning materials

Note:
- These figures come from Crashfree India's internal analysis and are included because they help show the institutional scale of the problem.
